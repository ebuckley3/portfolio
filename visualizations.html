The Long Fifties Seventeen Magazine Analysis
"Analyzing Teen Girl Culture Through Seventeen Magazine's AI Collages" captures my exploration of Seventeen Magazine covers from the "long fifties" (1945-1965) as a way to understand shifts in media representation for teen girls. Using AI to collage, classify, and analyze design elements, I uncovered patterns in cover imagery and demographics, which remained surprisingly consistent. The AI’s interpretation revealed "noise" in distorted text and a visual jumble, adding another layer to the study of AI's limitations in accurately reading and preserving cultural artifacts. Through visualizations, I found all models on the covers to be white, reflecting the limited diversity of the time. The AI’s breakdown of themes and model demographics provided insights that invite further research on the magazine’s influence and how it framed gender, beauty, and social expectations for young women. While archival access is costly, this project has fueled my curiosity to investigate historical messages Seventeen conveyed to girls about topics such as college, relationships, and sexuality.
